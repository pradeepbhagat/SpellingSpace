# Spelling Space 🚀

A gentle, multi-sensory spelling game built for a 6-year-old who knows sounds but
can't yet read words. It turns the school's "2 words a day, dictation on Wednesday"
routine into a fun space adventure.

## How to run
1. Open **index.html** in any modern browser (Chrome, Edge, or Safari).
   - On a tablet: copy the `SpellingSpace` folder to the tablet and open `index.html`,
     or host it (see below) and open the link.
2. Turn the **volume on** — the app talks and makes sounds.
3. Tap once anywhere first (this "unlocks" sound on tablets/phones).

## Daily routine (30 seconds for the grown-up)
1. Tap **👨‍🚀 Grown-up**.
2. Type today's 2 school words, press **Add** for each. An icon is picked
   automatically — tap **change** if you want a different one.
3. Hand the tablet to your child and tap **▶ Play Today's Words**.

## What your child does (each word, 4 easy steps)
1. **Listen & See** 👀 — hears the word; letters light up one sound at a time.
2. **Tap the sounds** 👆 — taps each letter in order (builds letter–sound link).
3. **Build the word** 🧩 — drags shuffled letter tiles into place (picture + audio hints).
4. **Dictation** 🎧 — hears the word only, then spells it from scratch — just like the real test.

Every correct letter says its name and makes a happy sound. Finishing a word
= confetti, a rocket cheer, and ⭐ stars. A wrong tile shakes gently and the app
re-plays the sound to help him listen — never a harsh "wrong".

## Built to prevent "fluke" wins (focus, not luck)
Kids often beat drag-and-drop games by randomly dropping tiles until something
sticks — finishing without learning. Spelling Space is designed so that can't happen:
- **One letter at a time, in order.** Only the *next* slot is active (glowing); the
  rest are dimmed. He has to think "what comes **next**?" instead of shape-matching
  anywhere.
- **Wrong drops pause the game.** A wrong letter shakes, buzzes, and briefly **locks
  the tiles** while the target sound replays — this breaks the rapid-guessing rhythm
  and forces him to *listen*.
- **Escalating help, not free answers.** First wrong: "Which letter says /X/?" Second
  wrong: the correct tile glows — but he still has to place it himself (guided, not
  fluked).
- **Rewards depend on focus.** A clean first-try dictation earns ⭐⭐⭐; guessing earns
  fewer stars, and **only a focused (near-clean) dictation counts toward mastery**.
- **Too much random guessing restarts the word** gently ("Let's try again together"),
  so a messy scramble can never fluke a win.

## Motivation built in
- 🛸 A friendly alien buddy that hops, cheers, and encourages as he plays.
- 🔥 Streak combos — several correct letters in a row trigger a "3 in a row!" cheer.
- ✨ Sparkle bursts when a letter lands correctly.
- 🚀 A rocket blasts off at the end of a session.
- 🌠 Shooting stars drift across the space background.
- ⭐ Stars for every word completed.
- 🏆 Badges unlock at 5, 15, 30, 50, 80, 120 stars (Moon → Galaxy).
- 🚀 A "fuel bar" fills up through the session.

## Smart practice (how words are scheduled)
- **Every word is saved forever** on the device — nothing is ever dropped.
- **New words are top priority for a few days.** A freshly added word stays "New 🔥"
  and leads every practice session (appearing twice per session) until it's mastered.
- **Older words are woven in between** the new ones for review, so nothing is forgotten.
  The least-recently-practised and most-mistaken words come up first.
- A word becomes **Mastered ✓** only after he spells it correctly in the dictation
  stage on **3 different days** — so a new word genuinely stays in rotation for a few
  days before it steps aside.
- Mastered words still resurface occasionally to stay fresh.
- The grown-up screen shows a live summary: how many words are saved, new, in review,
  and mastered.
- Progress saves automatically on the device (no internet or login needed).

## Tips for parents
- Sit with him the first few times and cheer — your reaction is the biggest reward.
- Keep sessions short (5–10 min). Stop while it's still fun.
- Use **🔊 Hear word** and **💡 Hint** freely; struggling then getting help is how
  learning sticks. There is no penalty.
- In **Grown-up → Voice**, pick the clearest English voice on your device.

## Hosting it (optional, so it's just a link)
Because it's a single file, you can host it free:
- **GitHub Pages**: put `index.html` in a repo, enable Pages.
- Or any static host (Netlify drag-and-drop, etc.).
Then bookmark the link / add it to the tablet home screen.

## Privacy
Everything stays on the device (browser localStorage). No accounts, no tracking,
no data leaves the tablet.

## Notes / limits
- Uses the browser's built-in text-to-speech. Voice quality depends on the device.
  Tablets and modern phones usually sound good.
- Best for short, phonetic words (3–6 letters), which matches early spelling lists.
